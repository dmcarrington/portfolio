About Me

From corporate blockchain to freedom tech

The Short Version

I am a Chartered Engineer and software engineering leader with 20+ years of professional experience, now building open-source freedom technology full-time. Based in Northwest England, I focus on creating tools for regular people — not fintech centres or venture-backed startups.

The Journey

I spent two decades in the corporate software and engineering world, including a role as Software Engineering Manager at Everledger working on blockchain and distributed ledger technology. I hold Chartered Engineer status (CEng MIET) through the Institution of Engineering and Technology and Chartered Manager status (CMgr) through the Chartered Management Institute.

I got into Bitcoin in 2017 and started building and running various types of nodes, excited by being able to take control of my own assets and the thought of being able to send money permissionlessly. Since then, I have gone deeper — building signing devices, deploying self-sovereign tools, and reducing my dependency on big tech wherever possible.

Along the way I picked up enterprise blockchain experience with Hyperledger Fabric, Ethereum smart contracts, and IPFS-based file transfer systems. I completed blockchain certifications through the Linux Foundation, edX, and Udemy, and learned Rust to get closer to the protocol-level work I care about.

What I Am Building Now

My current work is centred on the Nostr protocol and the Bitcoin Lightning Network. I am building native Android applications in Kotlin with Jetpack Compose, using the rust-nostr SDK to implement NIPs from the ground up. My projects span photo sharing (Unfiltered), decentralised photo marketplaces (Zap Gallery), social productivity tools (Time Tribe), and AI tooling (Nostr MCP Server).

On the hardware side, I ported the Bowser Bitcoin wallet to the LILYGO T-Watch as Bitwatch — a signing device hidden in plain sight on your wrist. I 3D print enclosures for nodes and signing devices, and I am building a REST API for Bambu printers with plans for Nostr DVM integration to enable decentralised manufacturing workflows.

What I Am Looking For

I want to put my experience to use helping freedom tech projects ship software. I bring professional-grade engineering discipline — architecture, testing, CI/CD, team leadership — to open-source projects that are building sovereignty-preserving tools. Whether it is Nostr client development, Bitcoin tooling, Lightning integrations, or something else in this space, I am ready to contribute.

My focus is entirely on creating tools for regular people and small businesses to use, encouraging adoption and building local communities.

Get in Touch

I am looking for opportunities to contribute to freedom tech projects full-time. Whether you are building on Nostr, Bitcoin, or any sovereignty-preserving technology — let's talk.
